# Glossary of Terms

This glossary provides definitions for key terms used throughout the AI/Spec-Driven Textbook for Physical AI & Humanoid Robotics.

## A

### Actuator
A component of a robot that converts control signals into physical motion. Actuators enable robots to interact with their environment by producing movement, force, or other physical effects.

### Affordance
In ecological psychology and robotics, an affordance is a possibility for action that the environment offers to an agent. For example, a handle affords grasping, a ramp affords climbing.

### AI-Physical System Interaction
The process by which artificial intelligence systems perceive, reason about, and act upon the physical world, creating a feedback loop between digital intelligence and physical reality.

### Algolia DocSearch
A search functionality powered by Algolia that enables users to search across documentation sites, providing instant and relevant search results.

## B

### Breadcrumb Navigation
A navigational aid that shows the user's location in a website or application hierarchy, typically displayed as a horizontal bar with links separated by chevrons.

### Bayesian Network
A probabilistic graphical model that represents a set of variables and their conditional dependencies via a directed acyclic graph, used for reasoning under uncertainty.

## C

### Chapter Navigation
A user interface component that provides links to the previous and next sections or chapters, facilitating sequential reading of content.

### Configuration Space (C-space)
The space of all possible configurations of a robot, where each point represents a unique arrangement of the robot's joints or other controllable parameters.

### Control System
A system that manages, commands, directs, or regulates the behavior of other devices or systems using control loops to achieve desired outputs.

### Cyber-Physical System
A system with both computational and physical elements, where physical processes are monitored and controlled by computer-based algorithms.

## D

### Docusaurus
A modern static website generator for building documentation websites, providing features like search, versioning, and internationalization out of the box.

### Embodied Cognition
The theory that cognitive processes are deeply rooted in the body's interactions with the environment, suggesting that the body influences the mind.

## E

### Embodiment
The concept that intelligence depends on the body's physical form and its interactions with the environment, not just on abstract computation.

### Environment
The physical or simulated world in which a robot or AI system operates, including all objects, surfaces, and conditions that affect its sensors and actuators.

## F

### Feedback Control
A control system that uses sensor information to adjust actuator commands and correct for errors, ensuring the system behaves as intended.

### Forward Kinematics
The process of calculating the position and orientation of a robot's end-effector based on the known joint angles.

## G

### Gaussian Process
A collection of random variables, any finite number of which have a joint Gaussian distribution, used in machine learning for regression and classification tasks.

### Gazebo
A 3D simulation environment for robotics that provides accurate physics simulation and realistic rendering.

## H

### Human-Robot Interaction (HRI)
The study of interactions between humans and robots, focusing on design, development, and evaluation of robots for human use.

### Humanoid Robot
A robot with a body structure similar to that of a human, typically featuring a head, torso, two arms, and two legs.

## I

### Inverse Kinematics
The process of calculating the required joint angles to achieve a desired position and orientation of a robot's end-effector.

### Inertial Measurement Unit (IMU)
An electronic device that measures and reports a body's specific force, angular rate, and sometimes the magnetic field surrounding the body, using a combination of accelerometers, gyroscopes, and magnetometers.

### Infima
A CSS framework designed to work well for content-centric websites, used by default in Docusaurus themes.

## K

### Kinematics
The branch of mechanics that deals with pure motion, without reference to the masses of the objects or the forces producing the motion.

## L

### LiDAR
Light Detection and Ranging - a remote sensing method that uses light in the form of a pulsed laser to measure distances to the Earth, commonly used in robotics for mapping and navigation.

### Learning Outcome
A clear, specific statement of what the learner will be able to do after completing a learning activity, typically following the format: "By the end of this [chapter/module], students will be able to [ACTION VERB] [CONTENT] [CONTEXT]."

## M

### Machine Learning
A type of artificial intelligence that enables computers to learn and make decisions from data without being explicitly programmed for every action.

### Markov Decision Process (MDP)
A mathematical framework for modeling decision-making in situations where outcomes are partly random and partly under the control of a decision maker.

### Mobile-Responsive Design
A design approach that ensures web content displays properly on various devices and screen sizes, adapting layout and functionality as needed.

### Motion Planning
The computational problem of automatically planning a path for a robot or other moving object from a starting position to a goal position while avoiding obstacles.

### Morphological Computation
The idea that the physical body of an agent can perform computations that would otherwise need to be done by the controller, exploiting the body's physical properties for intelligent behavior.

## N

### Navigation Mesh
A data structure that represents the walkable areas of an environment, used by pathfinding algorithms to find valid routes.

### Next/Previous Navigation
A user interface pattern that allows users to navigate sequentially through content by providing links to the adjacent items in a sequence.

## P

### Particle Filter
A recursive Bayesian estimation algorithm commonly used for tracking and state estimation, representing the posterior distribution of a state using a set of random particles.

### Path Planning
The computational process of finding a valid and optimal path from a starting point to a destination while avoiding obstacles.

### Perception System
The component of a Physical AI system that processes raw sensor data to extract meaningful information about the environment.

### Physical AI
A field that combines artificial intelligence with physical systems to create machines that can perceive, reason, and act in the physical world, contrasting with traditional AI that operates primarily in digital domains.

### PID Control
Proportional-Integral-Derivative control - a control loop feedback mechanism widely used in industrial control systems to minimize the error between a desired setpoint and a measured process variable.

### Probabilistic Model
A mathematical framework that represents uncertainty explicitly and allows systems to make optimal decisions despite uncertain information.

## R

### Reinforcement Learning
A type of machine learning where an agent learns to make decisions by performing actions and receiving rewards or penalties, learning to maximize cumulative reward.

### Robot Operating System (ROS)
A flexible framework for writing robot software, providing a collection of tools, libraries, and conventions for creating robot applications.

### ROS2
The second generation of the Robot Operating System, providing improved features for building robotic systems including better real-time support and multi-robot systems.

## S

### Sensor Fusion
The process of combining information from multiple sensors to create a more accurate and reliable understanding of the environment than would be possible with any single sensor.

### SLAM (Simultaneous Localization and Mapping)
The computational problem of constructing or updating a map of an unknown environment while simultaneously keeping track of an agent's location within it.

### State Estimation
The process of estimating the internal state of a system from noisy and incomplete measurements, often using filtering techniques.

### System Architecture Diagram
A visual representation showing the components of a system and their relationships, used to understand and communicate system design.

## T

### Template
A pre-formatted document structure that serves as a starting point for creating new content, ensuring consistency and proper structure.

### Trajectory Optimization
The process of finding time- and energy-optimal trajectories for robotic systems while satisfying constraints and avoiding obstacles.

## U

### Uncertainty Management
The ability of Physical AI systems to handle uncertainty in sensing, actuation, and environmental conditions, making robust decisions despite imperfect information.

### Utility Theory
A framework for making rational decisions under uncertainty, where choices are made to maximize expected utility.

## V

### Vision System
A component of a robot that processes visual information from cameras to extract meaningful data about the environment.

## W

### Waypoint
A reference point in physical space used for navigation, typically representing a location that a robot should move to or pass through.

## Z

### Zero Moment Point (ZMP)
A criterion for static and dynamic stability of legged robots, representing the point on the ground where the sum of all moments of the active forces is zero.
